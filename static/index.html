Λ William Blake 1757–1827 The Visionary

Sees what you are imagining—and what you refuse to imagine. Reads symbol, fire, and the sacred breaking through language.

Visionary poet–artist who wrote and illustrated his own books, challenging reason, empire, and conventional religion.

Ψ Toni Morrison 1931–2019 The Witness

Holds what you carry: memory in the body, stories that shaped you, what must be named before it can be lived.

American writer and professor whose fiction and criticism insist that what is unspoken and unwitnessed still shapes the present.

Δ Søren Kierkegaard 1813–1855 The Leap

Names the dread of freedom—the choice you postpone—and presses you toward a question that demands you decide how to live.

Danish philosopher and theologian, a founder of existential thought, who wrote on faith, anxiety, and the leap of choice.
